Ode to School Supplies

I love my school supplies
like really, REALLY love them
shopping for the start of new year
always brings me joy

I’ll start with my pencils,
always mechanical when possible,
0.5mm, no more, no less,
and always an eraser separate.
From 7th grade to sophomore year,
I had the same pencil year after year.
Many times it was lost, but always found.
Bought from a japanese stationary store located in the US of A.
When my pink prized possession finally broke,
I felt like a part of me was gone
I tried continually to find the same one,
but sadly to no avail. I moved on
and found a new pencil online.
I learned my lesson, and
this time, I bought three.

Next, my highlighters.
I’ve always had them,
but not more than two or three.
For my final year, I swapped
my colored pens from new york
to new highlighters.
….
15 to be exact
….
Sorted in rainbow order,
each perfectly aligned.
Color-coding my notes,
or whatever I else I have in mind.

Finally, we have my graph stamp.
Purchased when I was in Algebra 2,
it made coordinate graphs,
with the click of a button.
It was a wonder,
questioned about by many classmates.
Sadly, as I moved up through AP Calc,
it just sat there, unused.
I still carry it with me,
incase I can finally use it once more.
